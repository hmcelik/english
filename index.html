Origins

After the French Revolution, the foundation of the nationalist rhetoric was sown among the populace which called for a unified nation consisting of one and one race only. Since at the time, all jewish communities throughout Europe were minorities in country consisting of other nationalities, they quickly became the target for atrocities and mass murder. The prominence of these movements depended on the state of the ever-changing nature of the French government at the time. France become a monarchy again after the conclusion of the Napoleonic wars, during which republican values were suppressed heavily and the calls for a unified ethno-state were rendered powerless. However, the monarchy collapsed after the revolutions of 1848 leading to the creation of the Second French Republic.

Rise

During the second republic and later the second empire(which were both led by the same person) the anti-semitic movements failed to gather much support as a direct result of Emperor Napoleon III’s favorable views towards them. However, another side of Fascism originated during the Second Empire. France’s overseas and European Imperial ambitions rose to an all time high during this era, culminating in the French Invasion of Mexico and the Franco-Prussian war. After the Franco-Prussian war which led to the downfall of the Empire and the declaration of the Third Republic, the French ideological mindset changed drastically. This change in views was partly due to the resentment the new government held towards Napoleon III and partly due to the loss of Alsace-Lorraine, a part of the french mainland. The desire to take the region back and find a new French Empire with the help of overseas territories led to the height of French Imperialism during this time, with the nationalist rhetoric spreading through political circles like wildfire. All of these laid the foundations for the fascist mindset for the coming years, and led to the French acquisition of territories such as most of Western Sub-Saharan Africa and Indochina. However, the most prominent and famous event that is remembered with the characteristics of this era is the Dreyfus Affair.

Dreyfus Affair

The Dreyfus Affair is a series of events leading to the French military high command accusing a low ranking Jewish officer for spying for the German Army despite having no proof of it whatsoever. The ensuing trials which took years only proved how deep the anti-semitic roots were in the army to the public and Alfred Dreyfus was found guilty at first and spent many years of his life in a prison, serving for a crime he had no connection to. However, despite this injustice, a portion of the public stood up for Dreyfus, which was the major reason for his pardon in the following years. Despite the considerable backlash from the public, this infamous series of events is shown to be one of the first stepping stone for the rise of French Fascism.

Height

The prominence of French Fascism was rather miniscule before the outbreak of WW2. With the fall of France to Nazi occupation, extremist right wing groups under the senior war hero Philippe Petain were brought to high ranking positions in the newly created puppet stated centered around the town of Vichy. This so called “French State” more commonly known by the public as Vichy France held no actual power and was rather used as a way for the Third Reich to have a form of control over the French people. While it was true that it didn’t hold any political or militaristic power(besides the paramilitary force called the “Milice”), the Vichy government worked as a tool to refine the French Fascist ideology which showed remarkable similarities to German Fascism, such as the call for the extermination of Jews and the formation of an ideologically and ethnically uniform state.

Downfall and Legacy

After the fall of the Vichy government in late 1944, the French Fascist movement went somewhat underground and was forced to evolve in secrecy under many varying political organizations, and its major opponent went from Jews and other “undesireables” to ethnicities such as Algerians and other Muslims with the oncoming wave of immigrations from former French colonies. However, the ideology seems to have somewhat resurfaced in these last years with the Syrian refugee crisis fueling anti-muslim sentiment in the country. The ideology seems to be rallying around Marine Le Pen in a more watered down version hiding behind the guise of “nationalism” . As of now, it is too soon to say what the future of the far-right movement will be in France.
